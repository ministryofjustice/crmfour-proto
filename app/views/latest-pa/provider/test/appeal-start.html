Appeal

Before you appeal

Do not appeal for:

  • more funding than you originally applied
  • funding for work already done
  • making changes to your application

If you need to make any changes, make a new application or explain the change at the billing stage.

What to include

Give a detailed explanation of the grounds of your appeal and upload supporting documents.

Explain why:

  • the expert's evidence is key to the case
  • the expert needs to be very experienced
  • the material is so unusual that few experts qualify to help

Relevant documents

Upload relevant evidence such as:

  • prosecution case summary
  • defence statement
  • proof of evidence
  • counsel’s opinion as to why the expert is needed
  • expert reports already obtained in the same or a related area of expertise for the same matter

What happens next?

An LAA caseworker will respond to your appeal. If you disagree with the decision, it can be sent to an independent costs assessor (ICA). The ICA’s decision is final (as stated in the Criminal Bills Assessment Manual (CBAM) rule 4.4.1).
